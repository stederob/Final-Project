Teapot

Since the very geometric shape of the teapot I decided to build a field for each element and rotate around the z axis.

I have therefore defined the form of a single sector of the teapot, through Hermite and Bezier curves and Coons patches, I then replicated the shape and colored it of two different colors: black and white. Then this figure is obtained by rotating the volume of the teapot.

var Su0 = CUBIC_HERMITE(S0)([[COS(PI/6),SIN(PI/12),0],[COS(PI/6),-SIN(PI/12),0],[TAN(PI/6),0,0],[0,TAN(PI/6),0]]);
var Su1 = CUBIC_HERMITE(S0)([[0.2+COS(PI/6),SIN(PI/12),1.9],[0.2+COS(PI/6),(-SIN(PI/12)),1.9],[TAN(PI/6),0,0],[0,TAN(PI/6),0]]);
var Sv0 = BEZIER(S1)([[COS(PI/6),SIN(PI/12),0],[COS(PI/6)+0.4,SIN(PI/12)+0.2,0.6],[COS(PI/6)+0.4,SIN(PI/12)+0.2,1.3],[COS(PI/6),SIN(PI/12),1.9]]);
var Sv1 = BEZIER(S1)([[COS(PI/6),-SIN(PI/12),0],[COS(PI/6)+0.4,-SIN(PI/12)-0.2,0.6],[COS(PI/6)+0.4,-SIN(PI/12)-0.2,1.3],[COS(PI/6),-SIN(PI/12),1.9]]);
var tp1 = MAP(COONS_PATCH([Su0,Su1,Sv0,Sv1]))(dom2D);

I have added the fund by rotating a circular arc (created using the function arc). I then generated through the lid of the sphere function and a bounded domain to PI/3 in height and PI/6 in width. Then I turned the sector achieved.

Finally I created the knob, the spouth and the handle through a patch of coons.
